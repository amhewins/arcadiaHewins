Graphic Design

Crisis Text Line

In the world today, we are always connected. So why not have getting help be just as instantaneous? We cannot turn our minds off so we only have problems 9-5. That is where the Crisis Text Line comes in! Using red goes along with the logo of the company, but also draws the attention of the passers-by. Examples of texts that one can send to the text line to show that you can send anything, there is no judgement, and that other people have the same problems. This poster was displayed around the Philadelphia University campus and Thomas Jefferson campus with their information displayed.

Sweetest Suite

Poster designed to be placed within residence halls at Philadelphia University and included in emails sent to the campus. A big driving force to get college students to do things is to offer prizes in the form of food or money. This competition offered a monetary prize in the form of gift cards so that played a big part in the design. All images were sumbitted in years past to showcase what the Office of Residence Life would look for.

Sex Degrees of Seperation

Designed for the Health Services of Philadelphia University for use within sexual health information sessions and handouts.

Laundry Infographic for Non-English Speaking Students

Philadelphia University hosts many students from outside the country who may not know how our laundry machines work. This infographic is meant to show a person who speaks any language how to do laundry in the machines provided.

Sexual Health Infographic

Uses typography to quickly and easily get four messages acrossed for sexual health information. Posted around the campus of Philadelphia University, mostly focused towards first year students to make them think.

Office of Residence Life Shirt Design

Shirt design for the Resident Assistants to wear during the first couple days of first year arrival. Bright blue color used to stand out in a sea of people. The 'ORL' on the front of the shirt is made up of words that relate to being apart of the Office of Residence Life family.

Get Active Infographic

There are so many opportunities to get outside and get active within Philadelphia. It is easy to simply assume because it is a major city that that is not the case, so the job of the banner was to prove that. Collaboration with a geospatial analyst brought this banner to life with a map that easily shows all active parts of Philadelphia. Calm tones were used to invite any personailty to view and use the content shown. The green was used to link a brain to grass and greenery outside beacuse the banner was designed with a display indoor in mind.
